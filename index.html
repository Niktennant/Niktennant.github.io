About Me

Pioneering a path from History to Computational Biology, fuelled by Data Science and Deep Learning

I hold an MSc in Data Science and a BA in History with a minor in Data Science both from Brown University.

I've applied my skills in several impactful projects, including the development of 'SenID', a revolutionary deep-learning methodology for cellular senescence identification. This project received recognition in a Hackathon, placing second amongst over 225 participants and a research paper based on my SenID project will be published next year.

My professional journey includes experience as an Incoming Data Scientist at Dunnhumby and a research assistant role at Singh Lab: Deep Learning Genomics Lab, where I contributed to cutting-edge research at the intersection of deep learning and genomics. I also served as a Project Team Lead at Brown University Data Science and was a Machine Learning Research Assistant at Brown University.

I'm fluent in English, conversational in French, and proficient in Python, SQL, R, Tensorflow, and Adobe Creative Cloud. Beyond my professional interests, I am a big fan of Lacrosse and Football (Soccer), and I enjoy keeping up with advancements in AI, Robotics, and Deep Learning.

I'm proud of the work I've done and excited about what's next.

Lastly, as a holder of dual US/UK citizenship, I am authorized to work for any employer in these countries.
